Recommendation (March 2016)

Final recommendations for Moriond.

  • in CVMFS under the xAODBTaggingEfficiency calibration area (March10_v1) - link - /cvmfs/atlas.cern.ch/repo/sw/database/GroupData/xAODBTaggingEfficiency

Recommendations

  • analyses applying flavour tagging on R=0.2 track-jets or calibrations stored outside the default folders are encouraged to switch to these recommendations.
  • analyses strongly dependent on the c-jet and light-jet uncertainties should not include these uncertainties when profiling. These uncertainties are still based on pre-recommendations and are therefore dominated by one nuisance parameter correlated across jet pT. Removing the leading c- and light-jet nuisance parameter when profiling can be achieved by evaluating the impact of +1 and -1 sigma variations onto the final analysis observable and by adding the uncertainty related to it directly to the final observable with an additional smearing term in the likelihood. If impractical, an alternative correlation model should be tested where the first c- and light-jet nuisance parameters are split in several jet pT bins (depending on the jet pT spectrum in the analysis) and left as uncorrelated. These recommendations were made using the JetEtMiss group's jet energy scale pre-recommendations, but are still valid for analyses using the JetEtMiss 'Moriond' updates released in February 2016. They are also valid for both the pre-recommendations and Moriond update for the jet vertex tagger, i.e. either selection requiring JVT>0.64 for jets with pT0.59 for jets with pT<60 GeV. The differences in b-tagging performance for these two selections are at the sub-percent level for b-jets, as shown in this talk.
